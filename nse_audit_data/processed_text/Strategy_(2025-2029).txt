1. Executive Summary 
The Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) is embarking on a visionary and transformative strategy designed to 
revolutionize Kenya’s capital markets and position the NSE as a leading force in Africa’s financial landscape. At 
the core of our strategy are ambitious and bold targets that align with our purpose of “Inspiring Africa’s 
Transformation” and our commitment to unlocking the full potential of the African continent through innovative 
market solutions. 
Strategic Direction and Goals 
Our strategic plan focuses on achieving several audacious goals that will drive both market growth and 
operational excellence:  
1. Expanding the Retail Investor Base: We aim to bring 9 million active retail investors into the market. This 
bold move will democratize access to capital markets, giving millions of Kenyans the opportunity to 
build wealth through investments. We will accomplish this by enhancing financial literacy, simplifying 
the investment process, and leveraging digital technologies to make markets more accessible to 
everyday Kenyans. Additionally, the exchange will recruit at least 500 NSE agents to ensure that we 
reach underserved Kenyans in every part of the country.  
2. Increasing the Number of Listed Companies: With a target to list 40 new companies, the NSE is committed 
to attracting both large and small enterprises, including SMEs, to the exchange. By listing these 
companies, we will deepen market liquidity, provide a wider array of investment opportunities, and 
support the growth of local businesses. This will be achieved through campaigns that highlight the 
benefits of listing and through regulatory innovations that reduce barriers to entry for smaller firms. 
3. Achieving Operational Efficiency: We are working towards a cost-to-income ratio of 40%, signifying a 
drive for operational excellence. By optimizing processes and utilizing cutting-edge technology, we aim 
to deliver services more efficiently and sustainably, ensuring long-term financial viability. Achieving 
operational efficiency will also call for review and re-engineering of processes and the structure as well 
as placement of the right people in the right jobs with the right tools and support to serve different client 
segments.  
4. Becoming a Top Employer: NSE’s internal transformation will focus on becoming a Top 20 Employer of 
Choice in Kenya. Through leadership development, a strong corporate culture, and employee 
engagement, we will attract top-tier talent, positioning ourselves as a hub for innovation and excellence 
in the financial sector. 
5. Diversifying Investment Products: We plan to list 50 diverse funds, including exchange-traded funds (ETFs), 
real estate investment trusts (REITs), and other innovative products. This will allow our investors to 
diversify their portfolios and participate in both traditional and alternative investments, appealing to a 
wide range of risk appetites and investor profiles. 
6. Building a Beloved Brand: The NSE aims to become one of the Top 10 Most Loved Brands in Kenya, 
leveraging on our values of Integrity, Excellence, Leadership, Boldness, and Innovation. We will build 
trust and brand loyalty through transparent practices, bold leadership, and consistent delivery of value 
to all market participants. 
 Purpose, Vision, and Mission 
Our strategic goals are firmly rooted in foundational statements: 
- Purpose: “Inspiring Africa’s Transformation” – Our role goes beyond market transactions; it is about contributing 
to the socio-economic growth of Africa by creating opportunities for businesses and individuals to thrive. 
- Vision: “To be the preferred value creation partner” – We seek to build enduring partnerships with businesses, 
investors, and stakeholders, focusing on long-term value creation and mutual growth. 
- Mission: “Unleashing Africa’s potential through innovative capital markets solutions powered by People, Partnerships, 
and Technology” – This mission underscores our commitment to harnessing the power of technology, human 
talent, and strategic collaborations to drive innovation and unlock the continent’s vast economic potential.

2. Background 
2.1. 
Background: Key Considerations Behind the Strategy 
Our bold strategy to transform Kenya’s capital markets through increased retail participation, new listings, and 
innovative financial products is grounded in a thorough understanding of the current economic, social, and 
technological landscape. Both macro and micro-level factors were instrumental in shaping this ambitious 
strategy. These considerations, ranging from global economic trends to local market dynamics, provide the 
foundation upon which our strategy is built. 
Macro-Level Considerations 
1. Global Economic Shifts and Africa’s Potential  
- 
Emerging Markets Focus: Globally, investors are increasingly turning their attention to emerging markets, 
with Africa being seen as a key frontier for growth. The continent’s rapidly expanding middle class, coupled 
with significant natural resources and technological advancements, presents an opportunity for capital 
markets to support economic growth and attract foreign investment. 
- 
COVID-19 Recovery and Resilience: The global economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic has reshaped 
investor preferences toward more resilient and diversified portfolios. Capital markets, especially in 
emerging economies like Kenya, need to provide a range of investment options to meet growing demand 
for resilience through diversified asset classes, including technology and sustainability-linked investments. 
- 
Sustainability and ESG Trends: There is a growing shift toward Environmental, Social, and Governance 
(ESG) - aligned investments. Institutional investors and funds are increasingly demanding sustainable 
investment vehicles. This global trend has created an opportunity for the NSE to develop green finance 
products, including green bonds and impact investment instruments, which align with global investor 
priorities. 
 
2. Technological Advancements and Digital Transformation  
- 
FinTech and Digital Innovation: The rise of financial technology (FinTech) has dramatically transformed 
how financial markets operate. Digital trading platforms, blockchain technology, and the use of artificial 
intelligence in market analytics are now essential elements of modern exchanges. Our strategy aims to 
harness technology is in response to this global shift, ensuring that it remains competitive in an increasing 
digital market. 
- 
Mobile Penetration in Africa: With the widespread adoption of mobile technology across Africa, Kenya has 
become a global leader in mobile banking and digital finance. This technological infrastructure provides a 
solid foundation for the NSE to attract retail investors through mobile-based trading platforms, expanding 
access to the capital markets for millions of individuals who previously lacked the tools to participate. 
 
3. Regional and Global Integration of Capital Markets 
- 
Africa’s Integration into Global Capital Markets: The African Continental Free Trade Agreement (AfCFTA) 
aims to boost intra-African trade and investment. As African economies become more integrated, capital 
markets will play a pivotal role in facilitating cross-border investments and trade. This strategy considers 
the potential of regional integration, positioning us as a gateway for investors seeking exposure to the 
broader African economy. 
- 
Competing Exchanges: NSE is aware of the growing competitiveness within Africa, with exchanges like the 
Johannesburg Stock Exchange (JSE), Lagos Stock Exchange, and others vying for regional and 
international investment flows. As such, NSE’s strategic focus on innovation, technology, and efficiency 
aims to solidify its position as a leading exchange in East Africa, offering differentiated services that attract 
both local and international investors. 
4. Demographic dividend 
Africa is currently the youngest continent, with over 60% of its population under the age of 25. By 2050, the 
continent's working-age population (those between 15 and 64 years) is expected to grow to 1.1 billion people

These demographic changes will bring new investors with a different approach to investing.  
- 
Digital Natives and Tech-Savvy Investors: The new generation of investors in Africa is digital first. With 
widespread access to cell phones and the internet, young Kenyans are more likely to engage with 
mobile-based financial platforms. This presents a significant opportunity for the NSE to digitally engage 
retail investors through user-friendly online platforms that facilitate stock trading and other forms of 
investment based on real-time data, educational and trading resources.  
- 
Demand for Innovative and Impact-Driven Products: The younger generation of investors is not only 
looking for profit but also purpose. Millennials and Gen Z investors are increasingly concerned with 
sustainability and social impact, aligning their investment decisions with companies and funds that 
prioritize environmental, social, and governance (ESG) issues. NSE's focus on listing 50 diverse funds, 
including sustainability-linked products like green bonds and impact investment funds, is a direct 
response to this shift in investor preferences. 
- 
Access to Low-Cost Investment Products: With more first-time and small-scale investors entering the 
market, there is increasing demand for low-cost investment products that offer flexibility and lower risk. 
Exchange-traded funds (ETFs), real estate investment trusts (REITs), and other products that provide 
diversification at lower entry points are becoming more popular, especially among young investors who 
may be more risk-averse or have limited capital. 
 
5. Regulatory Environment and Government Support  
- 
Supportive Regulatory Framework: The Government of Kenya has taken steps to create an enabling 
environment for the capital markets through supportive policies and regulatory reforms. The Capital 
Markets Authority (CMA) has been working toward easing regulatory barriers for listings, introducing 
financial products, and enhancing market integrity. This environment supports the NSE’s goals of 
increasing listings and diversifying investment products. 
- 
Tax Incentives and Market Development Policies: Government-led incentives to promote the listing of 
companies, particularly small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), and favorable tax policies for 
investors and issuers, create a fertile environment for NSE’s growth strategies. Moreover, ongoing 
reforms aimed at deepening the financial market provide a solid foundation for the exchange’s 
development goals. 
6. Investor Sentiment and Retail Market Potential  
- 
Low Retail Investor Participation: Currently, retail participation in Kenya’s capital markets remains low, 
driven by limited financial literacy, access issues, and lack of trust in financial institutions. With an 
estimated 9 million Kenyans having the potential to invest but currently not engaging in the capital 
markets, the NSE sees significant potential in capturing this underserved market segment through 
investor education, simplified platforms, and accessible investment products. 
- 
Untapped Savings and Wealth Creation: Kenya’s high savings rates in informal sectors highlight an 
untapped pool of capital that could be redirected into the capital markets. By offering low-cost 
investment products and financial inclusion initiatives, the NSE can attract these savings into formal 
market channels, providing retail investors with a path to wealth creation while deepening market 
liquidity. 
 
7. Challenges Facing Kenyan Enterprises 
- 
Capital Access for SMEs: Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in Kenya form a sizable portion of 
the economy but face significant hurdles in accessing capital. The NSE’s focus on listing 40 new

The development of NSE’s strategy is informed by a blend of global trends, local market realities, and internal

Africa’s economic future. 
2.2. 
Performance of the Nairobi Securities Exchange Over the Last 
Decade 
Over the past decade, the Nairobi Securities Exchange (NSE) has experienced both peaks of robust performance 
and periods of decline, driven by various macroeconomic and microeconomic factors. The NSE’s key metrics, 
such as the NSE 20 Share Index, market capitalization, and the participation ratio between local and foreign 
investors, reveal the broader trends affecting the market. Understanding these metrics provides context for why 
NSE's new strategy is now focusing heavily on local retail participation. 
2.2.1. 
Key Performance Metrics 
1. NSE 20 Share Index: 
 The NSE 20 Share Index is a key performance indicator that tracks the price movements of the top 20 blue-chip 
companies listed on the exchange. Over the past decade, the NSE 20 index has fluctuated significantly, with its 
highest performance occurring between 2014 and 2015, where the index was near 5,000 points. This was driven 
by high investor confidence, strong corporate earnings, and favorable macroeconomic conditions. However, 
recent years have seen significant drops. In 2023, for instance, the index dropped to as low as 1,416 points, 
highlighting a 10% retreat from earlier in the year, and marking one of the worst performances among African 
exchanges. This has since reversed with the share index improving to 1861 as at the end of October 2024.  
2. Market Capitalization: 
 Market capitalization, which represents the total value of all listed shares, also followed a similar pattern. During 
its peak in 2015, the NSE’s market capitalization was well above KES 2 trillion. However, by 2023, investor wealth 
had fallen sharply, with a loss of about KES 498 billion, bringing the market capitalization down to KES 1.487 
trillion. Much of this decline has been attributed to factors such as foreign investor exits, depreciation of the 
Kenyan shilling, and a global economic downturn.

3. Foreign vs. Local Investor Participation:   
 
Over the last decade, foreign investors have dominated the trading activity on the NSE, particularly in high-cap 
stocks such as Safaricom, Equity Bank, and East African Breweries Ltd (EABL). However, foreign investment 
has been highly volatile. For example, in 2023, the NSE saw significant foreign outflows, particularly due to a 
weakened shilling and a flight to safety by global investors, resulting in a net foreign outflow of KES 18.6 billion. 
This trend was exacerbated by global economic uncertainties and rising yields in safer markets, like the U.S. In

3. Strategic Imperatives 
This chapter details our strategic ambitions expressed as our purpose, vision, and mission statements. The 
ambition is underpinned by our values and value propositions captured in our business model canvas. The 
value propositions outline what we intend to be world-class in, what we will be known for and what we will use 
to differentiate our services in the sector.  
3.1. 
Our Strategic Ambition 
3.1.1. Purpose Statement 
Our purpose statement is: Inspiring Africa’s Transformation,’ which conveys a bold vision to position the NSE 
not merely as a marketplace but as a catalyst for broader socio-economic progress in Africa. It includes:  
1. Empowering Economic Growth: By facilitating access to capital for businesses, we enable companies to 
grow, innovate, and create jobs. This drives economic empowerment across Africa, supporting 
sustainable development and helping economies become more resilient and less dependent on external 
aid. Our purpose statement calls us to start this Africa-wide growth in Kenya, where we will seek to 
empower economic growth at individual, household, private and public sectors.  
2. Encouraging Financial Inclusion: With a goal of bringing 9 million active retail investors to the market, we 
aim to democratize access to investment opportunities. By making the market accessible to more 
individuals, including those who traditionally have not invested, we will foster a culture of financial 
literacy, savings, and wealth creation among everyday Africans. 
3. Supporting Africa’s Businesses to Thrive: By listing 40 new companies, we aim to become a growth 
platform for African businesses of all sizes. Through capital raised on the exchange, businesses can 
invest in expansion, innovation, and sustainability, thus fueling sectors critical for Africa’s future—such 
as agriculture, healthcare, and technology. 
4. Sustainable Development and Innovation: The focus on technology and a commitment to offering 
innovative capital market solutions reflects our role in driving progress that aligns with Africa’s 
developmental priorities. This includes addressing challenges such as climate change, infrastructure 
gaps, and digital transformation, which are key for Africa’s sustainable future. 
5. Transforming Perceptions of African Markets: By aiming to become a Top 10 most loved brand in Kenya 
and a Top 20 employer of choice, we will demonstrate a commitment to building trust and a powerful 
reputation. This will attract global investors and solidify Africa's place as a viable and attractive 
investment destination, transforming global perceptions of African capital markets. 
“Inspiring Africa’s Transformation” represents our timeless aspiration to function as a pivotal force that drives 
change across sectors, communities, and economies throughout Africa, laying the foundation for a prosperous 
and self-sustaining future for the continent. 
3.1.2. Vision Statement 
Our vision statement is “To be the preferred value creation partner". Our vision statement emphasizes our goal 
to become the go-to platform for generating and maximizing value for all our stakeholders, including investors, 
businesses, and the broader economy. The vision entails:  
1. Building Trust and Reliability: By aiming to be "preferred," we are committing to building trust and 
becoming a reliable institution that stakeholders consistently choose over other platforms. This implies 
delivering a dependable, transparent, and efficient marketplace where investors, companies and 
government can achieve their goals with confidence. 
2. Maximizing Value for Investors: For retail and institutional investors, "value creation" means offering a 
broad spectrum of profitable investment opportunities and innovative products. This will include 
expanding asset classes, improving market liquidity, and reducing transaction costs, which would help 
investors achieve optimal returns on their investments.

3.1.3. Mission Statement 
Our mission statement is: "Unleashing Africa’s potential through innovative capital markets solutions powered 
by People, Partnerships, and Technology" reflects our dedication to driving Africa’s development by leveraging 
creative and advanced financial solutions. Here’s a closer interpretation of its components:

vast, untapped resources—its human capital, natural assets, and growing markets. We aim to be a 
catalyst for the continent’s growth by creating pathways for companies, investors, and communities to 
realize their full potential through the opportunities that capital markets provide. 
2. Innovative capital markets solutions: This phrase highlights our focus on introducing groundbreaking 
financial products, services, and market models tailored to Africa’s unique needs. Innovation includes 
digital trading platforms, new asset classes, socially responsible investing products, or financial products 
for small and medium-sized enterprises. These solutions will support growth, diversify investment 
options, and help address specific challenges such as limited access to funding. 
3. Powered by People: People are at the core of our mission. We will prioritize a skilled, knowledgeable, and 
customer-focused team that understands the diverse needs of African markets and stakeholders. 
Additionally, it points to our role in fostering financial literacy and inclusion, ensuring that a wider 
population can engage with and benefit from capital market opportunities. 
4. Powered by Partnerships: This element emphasizes the importance of collaboration. By working with 
government entities, regulators, stockbrokers, investment banks, custodians, fund managers, CDSC,  
other stock exchanges, financial institutions, development agencies, and private sector partners, we will 
achieve greater impact. Partnerships allow us to combine resources, share expertise, and expand our 
reach across Africa, enhancing our ability to develop resilient and inclusive markets. 
5. Powered by Technology: Technology is a critical enabler of innovation and expansion in modern capital 
markets. Through technology, we will enhance trading efficiency, improve transparency, and broaden 
access for retail and institutional investors alike. Advanced technology will also support data analytics, 
cybersecurity, and real-time reporting, which are essential for modernizing African markets and making 
them competitive on a global scale. 
This mission statement underscores our goal of becoming a transformative force in Africa by creating accessible,

3.1.4. Our Values 
The values of Integrity, Excellence, Leadership, and Boldness & Innovation set a foundation for our culture and 
guide how we operate to fulfill our mission and vision. Here is an interpretation of each value in the NSE’s context: 
1. Integrity: Integrity is central to building trust in the capital markets. For us, this means adhering to the 
highest standards of honesty, transparency, and ethical practices in all interactions and transactions. 
Integrity ensures that investors, companies, and partners can rely on us as a fair and trustworthy 
marketplace. It underscores the importance of regulatory compliance, accurate financial reporting, and 
protecting the interests of all stakeholders, fostering long-term credibility and reliability in the market. 
2. Excellence: Excellence reflects our commitment to delivering superior quality in our services, operations, 
and overall performance. This value drives us to consistently meet and exceed the expectations of 
investors, listed companies, regulators, and other stakeholders. Excellence can be seen in efforts to 
optimize processes, continuously improve technology platforms, and provide outstanding customer 
service. By setting ambitious standards, we aim to inspire confidence and attract both local and 
international participants to its marketplace. 
3. Leadership: As one of Africa’s leading capital markets, we embrace leadership to set the tone for growth, 
best practices, and innovation across the continent. This value encourages us to pioneer new initiatives, 
drive positive change, and be a role model for other African exchanges. Leadership also involves 
advocating for policies that promote financial inclusion, sustainable investment, and economic 
resilience. By taking a proactive stance in policy, market education, and innovation, we will position 
ourselves as a forward-thinking, influential entity in Africa’s financial ecosystem. 
4. Boldness & Innovation: Boldness & Innovation reflects our ambition to break new ground and adopt 
creative solutions that advance Africa’s capital markets. This value encourages us to take calculated 
risks, explore new investment products, embrace digital transformation, and adapt to the rapidly 
changing financial landscape. Boldness drives us to lead on initiatives that may be new or untested in 
the African context, while innovation ensures that these efforts are both strategic and sustainable. This 
value supports our mission to bring transformative changes to the continent’s markets and attract a new 
generation of investors. 
Together, these values position us as a resilient, forward-looking institution that serves as a dependable partner 
for investors and companies, promoting a vibrant and inclusive capital market ecosystem that is essential to 
Africa’s growth and transformation.

The key components of our business model are outlined below.  
1. Customer Segments: The primary customer segments are ‘Investors (retail, institutional, and government)’ 
and Issuers. This reflects a two-sided market where the exchange connects those looking to invest 
(demand side) with companies and entities seeking to raise capital (supply side). Additionally, we will 
aim to improve our services to the non-trading clients who include our tenants, consulting, and 
technology clients.  
2. Value Propositions: Our value proposition consists of credible and diverse investment opportunities, 
efficient and predictable access to capital, and tailored, timely professional services. We are committed 
to providing reliable, varied investment options and ensuring that capital flows smoothly to where it is 
needed, creating value for both investors and issuers. We will also build excellent service offerings for 
our non-trading clients to ensure that we grow revenues from these clients to 60% of the revenues 
generated by the NSE.  
3. Customer Relationships: We will maintain our relationships primarily through Investor Relations and 
Issuer Support. These services are designed to build trust and offer support tailored to the needs of 
investors and issuers, fostering a positive experience and engagement across all customer segments. 
4. Channels: We will reach our customer segments through Online Trading, DMA (Direct Market Access), 
Agents, and Intermediaries. These channels allow for efficient and convenient market access, ensuring 
that investors and issuers can participate in the market from various access points, both directly and 
through intermediaries. 
5. Key Partners: Our main partners include Government & Regulators, Market Intermediaries, Financial & 
Development Partners, Technology Solution Providers, and Exchanges & Associations. These 
partnerships are essential for regulatory compliance, market operations, technology infrastructure, and 
expanding access to markets. For example, collaborating with regulators ensures that we operate within 
legal frameworks, while technology providers enhance trading platforms and operational technology to 
drive efficiency.  
6. Key Activities: Our core activities include Product Development, Knowledge Transfer, Regulatory 
Compliance, Thought Leadership, Investor Education, and Investor Awareness. These activities enable 
us to support market growth, ensure a strong regulatory environment, and educate stakeholders. 
Product development and investor education are crucial for engaging new investors and introducing 
innovative financial products that meet market needs. 
7. Key Resources: Our key resources include Technology, Financial Capital, Human Capital, Marketplaces, 
Brand Equity, and Enabling Frameworks. Technology is essential for trading platforms, while financial 
and human capital drive operational effectiveness. Brand equity helps establish trust with investors and 
enabling frameworks (regulatory and operational) support market stability and compliance. 
8. Cost Structure: Our main costs include OPEX (Operational Expenditures such as tech, staff, and agency 
costs), CAPEX (Capital Expenditures), and Regulatory Capital. This structure shows a significant 
investment in technology and human resources to maintain efficient operations and compliance, which 
aligns with our focus on operational excellence. 
9. Revenue Streams: The NSE generates revenue through Trading Revenues (transaction fees from trading 
activities) and Non-trading Revenues (such as consulting services, data services and rental income). The 
diversification of revenue sources is crucial for financial stability and growth, reducing reliance solely on 
trading fees. 
Our BMC aligns with our focus on building a value-driven institution that supports economic growth by 
connecting investors with capital needs through a well-regulated, innovative, and accessible platform. The 
focus on partnerships, technology, investor education, and diverse revenue streams aligns with its mission 
to "Unleash Africa’s potential" and supports our vision of being the "preferred value creation partner." This 
model demonstrates a comprehensive approach to balancing operational efficiency, customer focus, and 
sustainable revenue generation, setting a solid foundation for our long-term strategy.
